# AI may become the next biggest polluter without regulations

<p style="color:salmon;font-style:italic;width:100%;border-bottom:1px solid;">Opinion</p>

*With AI requiring more and more liquid cooling and energy; AI could pollute more than other pollutants if regulations don't control the AI bubble*  

![XAI Colossus Data Center Compute Hall](https://www.servethehome.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/xAI-Colossus-Data-Center-Compute-Hall.jpg)
*Image Credit: Serve The Home*
<p class="dropcap opinion">The race to build ever-more-powerful artificial intelligence is happening at a rapid pace; but in the rush to dominate the AI landscape, some companies are leaving communities behind in a cloud of toxic fumes. In <b>South Memphis, Tennessee</b>, where Elon Musk's xAI has built the world's largest AI supercomputer, "Colossus"; residents have complained of the 150 megawatt facility that also takes 1.5 million gallons of water.</p>

The More Perfect Union Foundation made a report on the Elon Musk supercomputer in South Memphis showing how residents are having significant health issues from the gas the supercomputer burns  

<p style="font-size:x-large;text-align:center;margin:0;font-family:sanserif;color:salmon;"><i>“Elon Musk’s massive xAI data center is poisoning Memphis. It's burning enough gas to power a small city, with no permits and no pollution controls.”</i></p>
<p style="font-size:large;text-align:center;margin:0;font-family:sanserif;color:grey;"> More Perfect Union </p>
